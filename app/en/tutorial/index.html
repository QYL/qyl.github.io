Tutorial

Introduction

Thank you for being a Dialogue user. This article will briefly introduce you how to use Dialogue. First, we need to know that Dialogue is a subtitle reader and it does not come with its own content. Therefore, you have to upload subtitle files(.ass or .srt) you want to read onto the ** Dialogue app.

Upload Files

Before uploading a file, your device must meet two conditions: (1) your mobile phone and computer are connected to the same Wi-Fi network; (2) the mobile phone does not has any proxy.

If the above mentioned conditions are met, you can upload subtitle files to the app. Click the “+” button in the upper right corner of the Files page of the app, and the application will present a page showing the address of the file uploading webpage.

Enter the corresponding address (including the port number) in the address bar of a browser and start uploading files. When finished uploading, just dismiss the page and you will see that the files you just uploaded appear in the file list.

Subtitle Parsing

If you open the .ass or .srt subtitle file directly with a text editor, you will find that in addition to the subtitle content and timeline, there are a lot of special characters. These are actually some special effects and style codes. These symbols can make subtitles looking good when displayed in the video, but to the people who are reading the content theses characters are considered as noise. Dialogue will filter out all these complicated symbols. It’s like magic. Only the dialogues in the subtitle are preservedand and presented to the user. All the user needs to do is to click the subtitle file they want to read in the file list, and the application will automatically start parsing. It should be noted that it may take a few seconds for the first time, and almost no waiting after that.

BookMarking New Words

While reading, if you encounter a new word, you can press and hold to query its definition. This design eliminates the need for users to constantly jump between different applications during reading and improves the reading experience. In addition, you can also collect new words together with their contexts.

Customize the New Word’s Context

By enabling the context selector in the settings, a context selector will pop up when adding new words. You can use it to select the context in which the word is located. This can ensure that the sentence example is a complete sentence, not a part of the sentence.

Content Export

Dialogue supports exporting subtitle content to PDF files, while new words can be exported to PDF or CSV files. PDF or CSV files can be managed (downloaded, deleted) directly in the web interface for uploading subtitles, and files can also be copied or shared to other apps via the share sheet.

Wireless Upload

Dialogue Supports uploading subtitle files to the app via Wi-Fi. It is an easy task to manage the files.

Sync with iCloud

Dialogue supports iCloud syncing. You can drop your subtitle files in the Dialogue directory under iCloud Drive on your Mac, and those files will be automatically synced to your iPhone/iPad.

Import from Other Apps

Download subtitle files in other applications and call out the share sheet within the app. Then select Dialogue in the pop-up menu to import the subtitle file into Dialogue.

Fast Scrolling

A round button appears as you scroll through the page. Drag it to scroll quickly.
